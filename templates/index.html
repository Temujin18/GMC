About us

Gruzo Maternity Clinic (GMC) started its operations on August 2009 at Sampaguita Street, Colongulo, Surallah, South Cotabato. It is owned by Faith G. Ugbana, a registered midwife practicing the profession for eighteen years. It started with one bed capacity and it expanded to three beds in 2016.

Ms. Ugbana advocates that birthing homes must be brought nearer to the less fortunate, hence its location. She believes that this would help lessen the cases of infant and maternal mortality during giving birth. According to Ms. Ugbana, there is no better place to do this than to her own domicile. Barangay Colongulo is one of the 17 barangays in Surallah where the T’boli and the B’laan people live. GMC flourished in the first three years of its operation but its performance started to fluctuate in the years thereafter. There was also a significant fall in GMC’s performance in 2015 when major players in the market started to be accredited by the PhilHealth.

Subsidy given by the PhilHealth to its beneficiaries zeroes out patients’ bill. This caused a significant change in their behaviour towards services offered by accredited and non-accredited health facilities. This event is a major turn-around to GMC as it was forced to shift from 24/7 service hours to “only as scheduled” basis. The owner was also forced to look for another job to support the family’s needs since the clinic was not earning enough for the family.

GMC’s primary service is the normal spontaneous delivery which covers pre- natal check-up, post-natal check-up, and new-born screening. Other services include

family planning related services such as the selling of contraceptives (i.e. condoms, pills, and injectables; IUD insertion/removal; ligation referral, PAP smear, pregnancy test; ear piercing/holing; hemoglobin and UTI test).
